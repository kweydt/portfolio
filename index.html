A Bit More About Me

I was born and raised in a tiny town on the Flathead Reservation in Montana. Getting accepted to Stanford brought me down to the bay area where I spent many hours in the Product Realization Lab honing my design process and creating products.

An internship at a startup, Homestead, was my first exposure to the world of interaction design. They were acquired by Intuit where I spent the first 3 years of my career leading various product and design efforts within the small business division (QuickBooks Online, Customer Manager, Intuit Websites Mobile, etc.).

After a brief stint at Netflix (Qwikster - a story best told over a beer), I landed at Practice Fusion, where I spent the next year leading design efforts on a complex electronic health records product for the web and tablets.

I was spending some time consulting at various EdTech startups (Class Dojo, TenMarks, etc.) looking for the right fit for my next endeavor when I discovered Looksharp (called InternMatch at the time). What merely started out as a design side gig wound up being my home for the next 3+ years after I fell in love with the humble and thoughtful team and their vision. I've since grown from a product and design lead into a VP of Product at Looksharp where manage a small team of extremely capable individuals and direct our product and design strategy.

When I'm not laser-focused on solving critical design and product problems I'm usually found tinkering on my car or some other various project, concocting wild creations in the kitchen with my wife, exploring the natural wonders of the world, or catching up with great friends over a hoppy IPA or delicious Belgian brew.
